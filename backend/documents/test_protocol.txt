IVF Protocol Overview
IVF (In Vitro Fertilization) involves ovarian stimulation, egg retrieval,
fertilization in the lab, and embryo transfer.
Common medications include FSH injections for stimulation.
The luteal phase is supported with progesterone after transfer.
Success rates vary by age: 40% under 35, 25% at 38-40, 10% over 42.

Ovarian stimulation typically lasts 8-14 days.
Follicle monitoring is done via transvaginal ultrasound every 2-3 days.
Egg retrieval is performed under sedation when follicles reach 18-20mm.
Fertilization rates with ICSI range from 70-80%.
Embryo transfer is performed on day 3 or day 5 (blastocyst stage).
Blastocyst transfers have higher implantation rates (~50% under 35).

Endometrial preparation for frozen embryo transfer (FET):
- Natural cycle: monitor ovulation, transfer on day LH+5 (blastocyst)
- Artificial cycle: estradiol 2-6mg/day for 12-14 days, progesterone added 5 days before transfer

OHSS (Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome) risk factors:
- High antral follicle count (>20)
- AMH > 3.5 ng/mL
- Previous OHSS episode
Management: GnRH agonist trigger, freeze all embryos, cabergoline 0.5mg/day
